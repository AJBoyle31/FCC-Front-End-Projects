Thomas Jefferson

Author of the Declaration of Independence

Here is a timeline of Thomas Jefferson's life:

  • 1743 - Born at Shadwell, VA
  • 1760 - Thomas Jefferson attended the College of William and Mary
  • 1767 - Admitted to practice law before General Court
  • 1770 - Construction begun at Monticello. Shadwell burned.
  • 1772 - Married Martha Wayles Skelton. Daughter Martha born
  • 1774 - Wrote A Summary View of the Rights of British America
  • 1775 - Elected to Continental Congress
  • 1776 - Drafted Declaration of Independence. Elected to Virginia House of Delegates. Appointed to revise Virginia laws
  • 1779 - Served as Governor of Virginia for 2 years
  • 1783 - Elected delegate to Congress
  • 1784 - In France as Commissioner and Minister for 5 years
  • 1790 - Served as first United States Secretary of State for 3 years.
  • 1794 - Began commercial manufacture of nails on Mulberry Row.
  • 1797 - Served as United States Vice President for next 4 years.
  • 1801 - Served as United States President for next 8 years
  • 1803 - Louisiana Purchase concluded. Lewis and Clark expedition launched
  • 1809 - Retired from presidency and public life. Remodeling of Monticello and construction of dependencies largely completed
  • 1815 - Sold 6,700 volume library to Congress
  • 1826 - Died at Monticello on July 4th

    • "Nothing can stop the man with the right mental attitude from achieving his goal; nothing on earth can help the man with the wrong mental attitude."

    - Thomas Jefferson
